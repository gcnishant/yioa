We empower youth immigrants

OUR MISSION

Young Immigrants of America (YIOA) aim to provide support for young Immigrants to adapt to their new lives in America. We support students in various fields of study and work.

MISSION STATEMENT

Providing immigrant children with a comfortable environment both inside and outside the classroom, to foster relationships and improve social skills, and build confidence.

Holding presentations and activities at community centers and schools, to ensure easy access for the students. In addition, holding personalized mentoring sessions to provide support, counseling, and friendship for the students.

WHO WE ARE

A nonprofit organization devoted to enabling young immigrants to excel academically and socially.

A Timeline of The Luma Project

June 2024

New Beginnings

LUMA's Founder and current President, Rabin Bhandari, initially started this organization going under the title Young Immigrants of America, along with Board Members Saad Bentahar, Camila Chi, Anushka Dutta, Prisha Guruwacharya, and Arvin Singh.

Sept. 2024

ETE Collab

To reach a broader community, YIOA worked with Equality Through Education to introduce the organization and its mission. Working cooperatively with ETE's President, Oorja Munot, YIOA's board held a successful webinar that opened the door for new members to get involved with YIOA and their mission in the upcoming future.

Oct. 2024

Meet the Primes

YIOA Founders were invited to BCPS' Meet the Primes event. Students, organizations, prime contractors, and agency reps gathered to network and discuss future plans. YIOA was able to reach out to contractors and organizations they were interested in. They were also able to meet representatives already interested in helping YIOA start even bigger projects and collaborate together in the future.

Nov. 2024

UNICEF Collab

YIOA's first interactive meeting came November 14th, collaborating with Eastern Technical High School's UNICEF United to put together goodie bags for the bright students in the ESOL program at Gunpowder Elementary to reinforce the importance of education and curiosity.

Dec. 2024

BCPS Budget Meeting

In December of 2024, YIOA had the chance to speak at the BCPS Budget Meeting and made sure to emphasize the needs of ESOL students and their experiences with the impact of language barriers on education. Through more personal examples, YIOA was the spokesperson for immigrant and international students struggling in the classroom.

Feb. 2025

TABCO BLM Symposium

YIOA participated in the Teachers Association of Baltimore County's Black Lives Matter symposium, demonstrating our commitment to social justice and equality. This event highlighted the intersection of immigrant rights and racial justice movements.

April 2025

Villa Cresta CuCo

On April 8th of 2025, YIOA attended Villa Cresta Elementary School's Cultural Coalescence event, supporting their PTA in encouraging their young students to explore, embrace, and enjoy cultures different than their own.

April 2025

MAPISAC

Shortly after Villa Cresta Elementary's CuCo, YIOA shifted gears and made themselves known to the youth at the Maryland Asian Pacific Islander South Asian Conference (MAPISAC). Through MAPISAC, YIOA was able to inspire driven youth a part of Maryland's Asian and Pacific Islander community to support them in their mission.
